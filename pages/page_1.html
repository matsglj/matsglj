BarLift


BarLift sends you awesome deals from bars and restaurants around you.

A work in progress, BarLift is an iOS and Android app for students looking for local drink deals.

BarLift gained 1500 users within one week of its launch and was a result of more than 100 user interviews and usability tests.

  • 2015 - Present

Design and Research

BarLift started off as a text messaging service. In developing native mobile applications, we sought to create additional value and functionality for our users. Additionally, we created a web application for Bars to manage their own deals.

Interviewing and Prototyping

We conducted more than 100 interviews to understand the best features that students wanted in their application. A key design challenge was understanding what features students would value and interact with the most.

Sketches and Mockups

I utilized Illustrator and Photoshop to create mockups and assets for the application. I also took an active role in delivering content for iTunes and Google Play stores' app approval processes.

Service Design

On the Bar-side, we wanted to design a web application for managers to keep track of their venues, manage their subscription and send out deals. A first step in putting all of this together was creating a process flow chart.

User testing in POP

From sketches to wireframes, mockups to screenshots - using the POP online tool and iOS app helped validate design concepts on the go.

Marketing and Product Development

With a successful beta launch, we are continuing to develop app features and our interface.

App Beta Lunch

We partnered with Northwestern's Dance marathon for our launch. We set up a competition, donating to the event on behalf of the student group with the most downloads. Users were prompted to input student group affiliation in the app. We hosted live user numbers on our website, which garnered more than 3000 visits.

New Functionality

We're using a variety of interviews and research methods to best understand our users. With our Beta application, we are also able to conduct more thourough usability tests to ensure that the app experience is as inutitive and rewarding as possible.

Collecting Data

In our upcoming update, we've integrated a variety of data points to better understand how users interact with our app with Mixpanel. Additionally, we're able to A/B test a variety of in-app content as well as our push notification messages (and time sent) via Parse.

Sharing Data With Bars

In addition to setting up deals, Bars wanted access to a variety of tools: from simple feedback forms to keep track of their events, to advanced analytical tools to better understand how weather, sports events and time of year might affect customers.
