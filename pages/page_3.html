MedCap


Rethinking the prescription drug market.

In team with 5 other undergraduates, I designed and created an electronic prescription drug cap and an accompanying iOS application. Together, they monitor patient drug quantity and alert intake times.

Project Research

The US healthcare system loses $290 billion a year on patients not adhering to doctor prescription guidelines. MedCap targets the issue of drug non-adherence and misuse.

Market Research

The goal of MedCap is to seamlessly integrate modern technology into the traditional trust-based approach to healthcare between patients and physicians. Having shadowed doctors we understood there was huge business opportunity to innovate in this field, and our market data validated this assumption.

Competitor Analysis

Several pharmaceutical companies are trying to address the issue of medication non-adherence. To differentiate ourselves, we wanted to come up with an unparralleled user experience by creating both a pill capsule and an application.

Product Development

We raised the necessary funds for prototype development through a variety of competitions and pitches, and started working on our solution at Northwestern's Engineering shop.

Hardware Design

We utilized 3D printing, Machine tools and electronics such as an Arduino board to develop our physical prototype. We went through a variety of mechanical designs before settling on a mechanism that was cheap to manufacture and reliable

Software Design

Having mocked up a prototype applciation framework and design in Illustrator, we developed the application in Xcode. The app uses an online Parse database that communicates to the physical device wirelessly.
